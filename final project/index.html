What is beyond the rainbow?

Animal's can see different colors based on their number of cone cells in their eyes
In fact, many species can see light with frequencies outside the human visible spectrum.
Let's see how increasing the number of cones changes what you can see...

2 Types of Cones
Dichromats

Bat

Bats only have two types of cones and can only see blue, green and yellow.
Scroll down to see the Bat Vision spectrum.

3 Types of Cones
Trichromats

Human

Humans have three types of cones.
By combining these cone types, humans can see the all the colors in the rainbow.

4 Types of Cones
Tetrachromates

Reindeer

Reindeer have four types of cones. They evolved an additional cone to help them survive in the Arctic. This extra cone allows them to see ultraviolet light. Ultraviolet light helps them identify objects, like fur or urine, that blend into the snowy landscape.

Ultraviolet

5 Types of Cones

Butterfly

Butterflys have five types of cones. Scientists think the addiitonal cones perceive things of great ecological importance to these butterflies — such as sex. For instance, with eyes alert to the slightest variation in the blue-green spectrum, male bluebottles can spot and chase their rivals, even when they’re flying against a blue sky.

Ultraviolet

16 Types of Cones

Mantis Shrimp

Mantis Shrimp is the king of the cones! It has the most cones of any animal!
This animal lives on the bottom of the ocean floor. With 16 types of cones, the Mantis Shrimp can distiguish more specific colors and see different depths of color, including more depths of ultraviolet. This probably gives the predatory shrimp a speed advantage in distinguishing between different-coloured prey.
